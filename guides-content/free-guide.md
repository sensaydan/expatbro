# Pre-Departure Checklist

## Documents
- [ ] Passport valid for 6+ months beyond your planned stay
- [ ] Digital copies of all important documents (cloud backup)
- [ ] International driver's license (if you plan to drive)
- [ ] Vaccination records
- [ ] Travel insurance documentation
- [ ] Proof of onward travel (some countries require this)

## Financial
- [ ] Notify your bank of travel plans
- [ ] Get a no-foreign-transaction-fee credit card (Chase Sapphire, Capital One Venture)
- [ ] Set up Wise account for international transfers
- [ ] Have $500+ USD cash as emergency backup
- [ ] Know your ATM withdrawal limits

## Tech
- [ ] Unlock your phone for international SIMs
- [ ] Download offline maps (Google Maps, Maps.me)
- [ ] Set up VPN (ExpressVPN or NordVPN)
- [ ] Download translation apps (Google Translate with offline packs)
- [ ] Get a portable charger

## Health
- [ ] Get travel health insurance (SafetyWing, World Nomads)
- [ ] Fill prescriptions for 90+ days
- [ ] Get vaccinations (varies by country)
- [ ] Know your blood type
- [ ] Research hospitals in your destination

# Visa Overview

## Mexico
- **Tourist:** 180 days on arrival, no visa required
- **Extension:** Visit Migración office before expiration
- **Residency:** Temporary resident visa requires $2,500/mo income proof

## Colombia
- **Tourist:** 90 days on arrival, extendable to 180 days
- **Digital Nomad:** 2-year visa, requires $3,000/mo income
- **Extension:** Apply at Migración Colombia before expiration

## Venezuela
- **Tourist:** 90 days on arrival for most nationalities
- **Reality:** Banking is complicated, bring USD cash
- **Warning:** Check current travel advisories

## Brazil
- **Tourist:** 90 days on arrival, no visa for Americans
- **Digital Nomad:** 1-year visa, requires $1,500/mo income
- **Extension:** Apply at Federal Police (Polícia Federal)

# Packing List for Long-Term Travel

## Essentials
- 1 week of clothes (laundry exists everywhere)
- Universal power adapter
- Laptop + charger
- Phone + charger
- Portable battery pack
- Headphones (noise-canceling if possible)
- Basic toiletries (buy more locally)
- Small first-aid kit
- Sunscreen

## Skip These
- Formal clothes (you won't need them)
- Multiple pairs of shoes (one good walking shoe, one flip-flop)
- Guidebooks (use your phone)
- Excessive "just in case" items

## Pro Tips
- Pack in a carry-on if possible (easier for buses/flights)
- Dark colors hide stains better
- Quick-dry fabrics are your friend

# Banking Basics

## Before You Leave
1. Get a no-foreign-fee credit card
2. Set up Wise account
3. Have backup cards from different banks
4. Notify banks of travel

## In-Country Options
- **Mexico:** Cash widely accepted, OXXO for bill pay
- **Colombia:** Nequi app works well for foreigners
- **Venezuela:** USD cash is king
- **Brazil:** Nubank or Nequi, need CPF (tax ID)

## Money Transfer
- **Wise:** Best rates, works everywhere
- **Western Union:** Good for cash pickup
- **Crypto:** Works but check local regulations

# Safety Quick Reference

## General Rules
1. Don't flash expensive items
2. Use Uber/DiDi instead of street taxis
3. Stay aware of your surroundings
4. Keep phone in pocket, not hand
5. Learn "no thanks" in local language

## If Robbed
- Don't resist
- Hand over what they want
- Report to police for insurance
- Cancel cards immediately

## Emergency Numbers
- **Mexico:** 911
- **Colombia:** 123
- **Venezuela:** 171
- **Brazil:** 190 (police), 192 (ambulance)

# 10 Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Booking long-term housing before arriving** — Always see the place first
2. **Overpacking** — You can buy things locally
3. **Not learning basic Spanish/Portuguese** — Even a little goes far
4. **Using street taxis** — Apps are safer and often cheaper
5. **Flashing wealth** — Leave the jewelry at home
6. **Ignoring local customs** — Being late is normal, adapt
7. **Only hanging with other expats** — You'll miss the real experience
8. **Overstaying visas** — The fines and complications aren't worth it
9. **Not having backup payment methods** — Cards get blocked
10. **Expecting things to work like home** — Embrace the differences
